4.3 Dual-Task Gait Assessment

Dual-task assessment evaluates the impact of cognitive load on gait performance, revealing fall risk not apparent during single-task walking.

Dual-Task Gait Protocol:
Single-task baseline: Normal pace 10-meter walk
Cognitive dual-task: Walk while counting backwards by 7s from 100
Motor dual-task: Walk while carrying a cup of water
Manual dual-task: Walk while carrying two different objects

Analysis: Calculate dual-task cost = ((Single-task - Dual-task)/Single-task) √ó 100
Interpretation: >20% dual-task cost indicates high fall risk

10.2 Addressing Fear of Falling

Fear-Activity Spiral: Fear of falling leads to activity restriction (50% of older adults), which causes deconditioning and increased actual fall risk. Breaking this cycle requires addressing both physical and psychological components.
FIGURE 24: Fear of Falling Intervention Strategies
Cognitive Strategies: Thought challenging and reframing techniques
Behavioral Strategies: Graded exposure and activity pacing
Integrated approach to fear of falling: Cognitive restructuring helps identify and challenge catastrophic thoughts about falling. Graded exposure therapy gradually reintroduces avoided activities with appropriate safety measures and confidence-building progression.

Evidence-Based Fear Reduction Techniques

Effective Interventions: Cognitive-behavioral approaches reduce fear of falling by 25-40% and increase activity levels by 15-30%. Combining physical exercise with cognitive strategies shows superior outcomes to either approach alone.

  • Emergency response: What to do when a fall occurs

Family Support Impact: Research shows family involvement increases exercise adherence by 40-60% and significantly improves long-term behavior change maintenance. Family members should be included in education sessions when possible.

12.4 Long-term Follow-up and Sustainability

Sustainability Factors: Successful long-term fall prevention requires ongoing support, booster sessions, and transition to community-based programs. Effect size typically decreases 25-50% after structured program completion without follow-up.

Sustainability Strategies

  • Booster sessions: Monthly follow-up for 3-6 months post-completion
  • Community partnerships: Transition to senior centers, YMCAs, community programs
  • Peer support networks: Exercise buddies, group maintenance sessions
  • Technology integration: Apps, wearables for continued monitoring
  • Primary care integration: Annual fall risk screening and referrals
