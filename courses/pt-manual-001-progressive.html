1.1 Manual Therapy Assessment Framework

Evidence Foundation: Manual therapy assessment follows a systematic approach integrating subjective examination, objective findings, and clinical reasoning to determine appropriate intervention strategies. Research demonstrates improved outcomes when assessment guides treatment selection.

1.2 Physical Examination Priorities

Objective Assessment Sequence:
1. Observation: Posture, movement patterns, guarding
2. Active Range of Motion: Quality, quantity, pain provocation
3. Passive Range of Motion: End-feel assessment, tissue resistance
4. Accessory Motion Testing: Joint play, tissue mobility
5. Palpation: Tissue texture, temperature, tenderness
6. Special Tests: Specific to suspected pathology

1.3 Clinical Reasoning Models

Clinical Reasoning Process: Effective manual therapy requires integrating multiple reasoning strategies including pattern recognition, hypothetico-deductive reasoning, and narrative reasoning to develop optimal treatment plans.

Maitland's Clinical Reasoning Framework

  • Brick Wall: Established knowledge and clinical experience
  • Permeable Brick Wall: Flexible application based on patient presentation
  • Clinical Reasoning Categories:
    • Source of symptoms (anatomy)
    • Contributing factors (biomechanics)
    • Precautions and contraindications (safety)
    • Management and treatment (intervention)
    • Prognosis (expected outcomes)

2.2 Trigger Point Therapy

Clinical Consideration: Myofascial trigger points are hyperirritable spots in skeletal muscle associated with palpable nodules in taut bands. Treatment requires precise identification and appropriate pressure application to achieve release.

Trigger Point Treatment Protocols

TechniqueDurationPressureExpected Response
