Gray's Anatomy: Lumbosacral Plexus & Dermatomes

Henry Gray's Anatomy of the Human Body, 20th Edition (1918)

Source: Public domain images from Gray's Anatomy, 20th edition (1918). Illustrated by Henry Vandyke Carter (1831-1897). These classic anatomical plates represent the gold standard of anatomical illustration from the pre-digital era, known for their exceptional accuracy and clarity.
Plate 822
Plan of Lumbar Plexus
Diagram showing the formation of the lumbar plexus from L1-L4 nerve roots. The iliohypogastric (L1), ilioinguinal (L1), genitofemoral (L1-L2), lateral femoral cutaneous (L2-L3), femoral (L2-L4), and obturator (L2-L4) nerves are shown with their origins and distributions.
Clinical Relevance: The lumbar plexus is formed within the psoas major muscle. The femoral nerve innervates the quadriceps and provides sensation to the anterior thigh. The obturator nerve innervates the hip adductors. Compression at this level can cause anterior thigh weakness and sensory changes.
Plate 828
Plan of Sacral and Pudendal Plexuses
Diagram illustrating the sacral plexus formed from L4-S4 nerve roots, giving rise to the superior and inferior gluteal nerves, posterior femoral cutaneous nerve, sciatic nerve (tibial and common peroneal divisions), and pudendal nerve.
Clinical Relevance: The sciatic nerve (L4-S3) is the largest nerve in the body and innervates the hamstrings and all muscles below the knee. Sacral plexus lesions can cause foot drop (peroneal), weakness of plantarflexion (tibial), and sensory changes in the posterior leg and foot.

Posterior view of the lower extremity showing the segmental distribution of cutaneous nerves. Illustrates the posterior femoral cutaneous nerve distribution, sural nerve, and the dermatomal pattern of the lower leg and foot.
Clinical Relevance: Understanding dermatomal patterns is essential for localizing nerve root lesions. L5 radiculopathy typically presents with pain/numbness in the lateral leg and dorsum of foot. S1 radiculopathy affects the posterior leg and lateral foot.

Lumbosacral Dermatome Key

L1: Groin region
L2: Anterior thigh (upper)
L3: Anterior thigh (lower), medial knee
L4: Medial leg/foot, medial malleolus
L5: Lateral leg, dorsum of foot, great toe
S1: Lateral foot, little toe, sole
S2: Posterior thigh, popliteal fossa
S3-S5: Perineum, saddle region
