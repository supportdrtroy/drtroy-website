1.1 Systematic Neurological Assessment

Evidence Foundation: Comprehensive neurological assessment integrates multiple domains including motor function, sensory function, cognitive status, and functional capacity. Research demonstrates that systematic assessment improves diagnostic accuracy and treatment outcomes in neurological rehabilitation.

1.2 Sensory System Assessment

Sensory Testing Protocol:
1. Light Touch: Cotton swab, random pattern
2. Pinprick: Sharp/dull discrimination
3. Temperature: Hot/cold test tubes
4. Proprioception: Joint position sense testing
5. Vibration: 128 Hz tuning fork
6. Two-Point Discrimination: Spatial acuity assessment

Modified Ashworth Scale for Spasticity

ScoreDescriptionClinical FindingFunctional Impact
0 No increase in muscle tone Normal tone No interference with function
1 Slight increase, catch and release Minimal resistance Minimal functional limitation
1+ Slight increase, catch at end range Minimal resistance at end Slight movement difficulty
2 More marked increase through range Moderate resistance Movement possible but difficult
3 Considerable increase, passive movement difficult Strong resistance Significant functional limitation
4 Affected part rigid in flexion or extension Rigidity Severe functional impairment

1.3 Cognitive and Behavioral Assessment

Cognitive Domains to Assess: Attention, memory, executive function, language, visuospatial processing, and processing speed are critical for functional recovery and safety. Use standardized tools when possible for objective measurement and tracking progress over time.

Trail Making Test Visual attention, task switching 5 minutes Executive function assessment

2.1 Stroke Recovery Mechanisms

Neuroplasticity Evidence: Stroke recovery occurs through multiple mechanisms including resolution of diaschisis, recruitment of perilesional areas, and reorganization of intact neural networks. Task-specific training optimizes neuroplasticity and functional recovery.

Stroke Recovery Phases and Treatment Focus

Recovery PhaseTimelinePrimary GoalsTreatment Approach
Acute 0-7 days Medical stability, early mobilization Positioning, basic mobility, prevention
Subacute 1 week - 6 months Motor recovery, functional training Task-specific practice, strengthening
Chronic >6 months Optimization, adaptation Intensive training, compensation
Early Mobilization Guidelines: Begin mobilization within 24-48 hours post-stroke when medically stable. Monitor for orthostatic hypotension, fatigue, and neurological deterioration. Progressive activity advancement based on patient tolerance and medical status.
