2. COURSE OBJECTIVES

Primary Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, participants will be able to:

Domain 1: Clinical Assessment Skills

  1. Apply a systematic SOAP-based examination model for musculoskeletal evaluation across all major body regions
  2. Differentiate between subjective and objective examination components and integrate findings for comprehensive assessment
  3. Identify red flag symptoms requiring immediate medical referral, with emphasis on cauda equina syndrome and serious pathology

Domain 2: Regional Musculoskeletal Expertise

  1. Demonstrate knowledge of anatomical structures and biomechanical principles for cervical, thoracic, and lumbar spine regions
  2. Perform and interpret range of motion assessments including normal values and capsular patterns for all major joints
  3. Execute special orthopedic tests with understanding of their diagnostic accuracy, sensitivity, and specificity values
  4. Apply clinical reasoning skills to upper extremity conditions including shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand pathologies
  5. Evaluate lower extremity dysfunction using evidence-based examination techniques for hip, knee, ankle, and foot

Domain 3: Evidence-Based Practice Integration

  1. Interpret diagnostic test results using likelihood ratios and post-test probability calculations
  2. Utilize clinical prediction rules and validated outcome measures in decision-making processes
  3. Apply current research evidence to support examination technique selection and clinical reasoning

Domain 4: Professional Practice Skills

  1. Document examination findings using proper SOAP format and professional terminology
  2. Establish SMART treatment goals based on examination findings and patient-centered care principles
  3. Integrate patient values and preferences with clinical expertise and research evidence
  4. Demonstrate cultural competency and effective communication during the examination process

3. STATEMENT OF RELEVANCE TO PHYSICAL THERAPY PRACTICE

This course directly addresses core competencies required for safe and effective physical therapy practice as defined by the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA) and Texas licensing requirements.

Clinical Relevance

Musculoskeletal evaluation forms the foundation of physical therapy practice, with over 85% of outpatient physical therapy encounters involving musculoskeletal conditions. This course provides essential knowledge and skills that directly impact:

  • Patient Safety: Recognition of red flag symptoms and contraindications to treatment prevents adverse events and ensures appropriate medical referrals
  • Diagnostic Accuracy: Proper application of special tests and clinical reasoning improves differential diagnosis and treatment planning
  • Evidence-Based Practice: Integration of current research enhances clinical decision-making and patient outcomes
  • Professional Standards: Systematic examination approaches align with APTA Practice Guidelines and Texas PT licensing requirements

Regulatory Alignment

The course content specifically aligns with:

  • Texas Administrative Code Title 22, Chapter 811: Physical therapy practice standards and examination requirements
  • APTA Guide to Physical Therapist Practice: Examination components and documentation standards
  • CAPTE Standards: Evidence-based practice and clinical reasoning competencies
  • Federation of State Boards Requirements: Continuing education in core practice areas

Professional Development Impact

This educational activity enhances practitioner competency in:

  • Clinical examination proficiency across all major body regions
  • Evidence-based decision making and diagnostic reasoning
  • Risk management and patient safety protocols
  • Professional documentation and communication standards
  • Integration of current research into clinical practice
